# 00 — Philosophy

> "Beginners are always focused on what you do, but the focus should be more on how much you do." — Andrej Karpathy, [Lex Fridman #333](https://lexfridman.com/andrej-karpathy/)

## Why this exists

Most "productivity systems" sold to developers are **productivity porn** — beautifully designed, never used past week 2. They optimize the *feeling* of being organized, not the *output* of shipped work or the *health* that lets you keep shipping.

This system optimizes for one outcome only:

> **A multi-job dev (backend + Solana/Rust + interview prep) ships consistently for 2+ years without burning out.**

Not "optimal." Not "10x." **Sustainable.**

## Three constraints we refuse to violate

1. **Health is non-negotiable.** Sleep &lt;6h three nights in a row → all bets are off, see `04-failure-recovery.md`. The Van Dongen et al. (2003) data is unambiguous: 14 days at 6h time-in-bed = cognitive performance equivalent to 2 nights of total sleep deprivation, and **subjects don't notice**. ([PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12683469/))

2. **Effectiveness > efficiency.** Doing the *right* deep work for 90 minutes beats fragmenting 6h into Slack-interrupted chunks. Paul Graham's [Maker's Schedule](https://paulgraham.com/makersschedule.html) is the foundation: makers need half-day blocks; one mid-day meeting can destroy a whole half-day.

3. **Compound > burst.** Naval: ["All returns in life — wealth, relationships, knowledge — come from compound interest."](https://x.com/naval/status/1109704032204009473) A 1h/day Rust habit for 365 days ≫ 12h once a quarter.

## Three things we refuse to add

1. **No 50-widget dashboard.** Every page exists because it's used daily or weekly. If a metric isn't acted on, it's deleted. Karpathy's `karpathy.ai` is one HTML page. Aim for that energy.

2. **No gamification.** Streaks are for habit visibility, not dopamine. No badges, no XP, no "level up." DHH: ["Stop saying ASAP. We get it. It's implied."](https://x.com/dhh/status/584745462445518848) — same energy: stop manufacturing urgency.

3. **No "track everything" mindset.** Anything not actionable is noise. Calorie counting is out. Mood-tagging every hour is out. Sleep hours, exercise Y/N, deep work hours, MIT done Y/N — that's the floor. Everything else is optional.

## What this is NOT

- **Not life coaching.** No affirmations, no morning gratitude rituals as a system requirement. If they work for you, fine — keep them in your head, not in this app.
- **Not Stoic LARPing.** Cold showers and 4am wakeups are not in the protocols because the evidence is weak (cold cognitive benefit: [Muller 2021 systematic review](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8470111/) — 9 of 10 studies showed *impaired* cognition during/after exposure).
- **Not hustle culture.** 73% of devs report having experienced burnout ([JetBrains 2023](https://www.jetbrains.com/lp/devecosystem-2023/), corroborated by [Tulili et al. 2023 systematic mapping study, n=92 papers](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0950584922002257)). The 4-jobs-at-once dev who skips sleep is the modal burnout case.
- **Not anti-ambition.** The remote Solana/Rust job goal is taken seriously. The system is designed to make it *more* likely by 2027, not less.

## The two-skill operating principles

This codebase was built under two operating principles imported from `CLAUDE.md`:

- **Karpathy guidelines** — surface assumptions, simplicity first, surgical changes, goal-driven execution.
- **Research discipline** — every non-trivial claim cites ≥2 independent sources; popular claims that don't replicate are flagged.

Apply the same discipline to your weekly review. If a habit "feels right" but the evidence is weak, **drop it**.
