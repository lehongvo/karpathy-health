# Exercise protocol

> The strongest non-sleep cognitive intervention. Modest effect, but real and replicated.

## Hard rules

| Rule | Threshold | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Aerobic | **3×/week × 40-min Zone-2** (HR 65-75% max, conversational) | [Erickson 2011 PNAS](https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.1015950108) |
| Resistance | 2×/week, full-body, compound lifts | [Liu-Ambrose 2010](https://jamanetwork.com/journals/jamainternalmedicine/fullarticle/415534) |
| Daily floor | 7,000+ steps even on rest days | Epidemiological consensus |
| Failure threshold | 5 days no exercise → "Recovery Sprint" | [Northey 2018 BJSM](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28438770/) |

## Why these specific numbers

- **Erickson 2011 (PNAS)**: 40-min walks 3×/week × 12 months → +2% hippocampal volume in older adults; correlated with serum BDNF.
- **Northey 2018 meta-analysis** (39 studies): pooled g = 0.29 (95% CI 0.17-0.41) for exercise → cognition in adults >50.
- **Wilckens 2021 meta-analysis** ([source](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11497212/)) confirms hippocampal preservation.

## Recovery from a lapse (5+ days no movement)

- Day 1: 20-min walk. That's it.
- Day 3: 30-min Zone-2.
- Day 5: 40-min Zone-2.
- Day 7: resume normal 3×/week.

Don't double the volume. You'll get hurt and quit.

## What this protocol does NOT claim

- **HIIT > Zone-2 for cognition**: HIIT has metabolic advantages, but cognitive evidence favors consistent Zone-2.
- **Pre-workout supplements / creatine for cognition**: marginal evidence for healthy adults; not load-bearing.
- **"Morning workout is best"**: timing matters less than consistency.
