# Sleep protocol

> The strongest single-lever intervention. Skip everything else first.

## Hard rules

| Rule | Threshold | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Minimum sleep | **7h time-in-bed**, ≥5 nights/week | [Van Dongen 2003](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12683469/) |
| Caffeine cutoff | **8h before bedtime** | [Drake 2013](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3805807/) |
| Morning light | **10-30 min outdoors within 1h of waking** | [Wright 2013](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4020279/) |
| Failure threshold | 3 consecutive nights &lt;6h → cancel optional commits | [Belenky 2003](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12603781/) |

## Why these specific numbers

- **7h, not 8h**: 8h is the population recommendation; 7h is the empirically-validated floor (Van Dongen). Hit 7h consistently before optimizing for 8.
- **Caffeine half-life ~5h average, range 1.5-9.5h** ([NCBI Bookshelf NBK223808](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK223808/)). Slow metabolizers (~10-15% of whites) need a ~12h cutoff.
- **Drake 2013**: 400mg caffeine 6h pre-bed cuts >1h of sleep. We use 8h as a safety margin.

## What this protocol does NOT recommend

- **Tracking sleep stages from a wearable** as a primary metric. Consumer wearables are noisy; track *hours* and *consistency*.
- **Citing Walker's "&lt;7h doubles cancer risk"**: not supported by underlying data ([Guzey](https://guzey.com/books/why-we-sleep/), [Gelman](https://statmodeling.stat.columbia.edu/2019/11/18/is-matthew-walkers-why-we-sleep-riddled-with-scientific-and-factual-errors/)).
- **Blue-light glasses** as a sleep fix. Cochrane 2023: inconclusive. Worry about *content* not wavelength.

## When you've nailed this

You wake within ±15 min daily, no alarm needed by week 3-4. Energy slider trends 4-5/5 in mornings. PVT-equivalent: faster reaction, fewer "I forgot what I was doing" moments.
